Guided Question #1
What changed compared to the default static positioning? Try to give different values to top and left or you can change it to bottom, right.

In comparison to the static positioning, the relative positioning gave way for the element to move from its original position. By giving top and left values, it made somewhat of a margin to the element, moving it without changing the position of the other elements.

Guided Question #2
What happens when you scroll the page? Why does the footer behave differently from position relative?

When you scroll the page, the footer has a fixed position, staying at the bottom of your screen. Since the footer has a fixed position, it behaves differently from the relative, and instead of movinng with the page, it stays in place.
